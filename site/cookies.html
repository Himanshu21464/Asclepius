cookies · trackers · the actual list

The list is shorter than the policy.

Most "cookie policies" are 2,000-word documents that hide a long enumeration of trackers behind a banner you have to dismiss. This is the same disclosure stripped of the legal padding: the actual cookies and trackers asclepius.health uses, in plain English.

cookies set · trackers loaded · third-party scripts

What we do store

The site uses localStorage for two preferences. Both stay on your device, never traverse the network, and you can clear them at any time from your browser's site-data panel.

asclepius-theme localStorage Your chosen color scheme — "dark" or "light" — so the site doesn't flash the wrong palette on the next page load. Set when you click the moon/sun toggle in the status strip. Read by js/main.js.
asclepius-demo-* localStorage The playground at /demo persists the in-browser ledger you build so refreshing the tab doesn't lose it. The keys live under the asclepius-demo- prefix. Clearing them restarts the demo.

That is the complete list. We do not use document.cookie, IndexedDB, or session-storage anywhere on the site.

What we load from third parties

Exactly one over-the-wire dependency at runtime: the Google Fonts stylesheet at fonts.googleapis.com, which fetches the three font families the site is typeset in. Google's font requests do not set cookies on this origin (the fonts.gstatic.com CDN is cookieless by design), but they do reveal your IP and user agent to Google. If you block Google Fonts, the page falls back to system fonts and reads cleanly.

That's it. No analytics. No A/B testing. No marketing pixels. No CDN-served JavaScript bundles. No "essential" cookies, "performance" cookies, "functional" cookies. The site does not load any external script anywhere.

Service worker

We register a service worker (sw.js) that caches the site's static assets so pages you have visited keep working offline. The cache is local to your browser; nothing is sent anywhere. To remove it, clear "site data" or unregister the worker in your browser's application panel.

Why this matters

The product is a trust substrate. It would be incoherent for the site that markets it to extract behavioral data from visitors. We don't, and the absence is structural — the site cannot opt-in to analytics without a code change, a PR, and a release tag.

If you'd like the same posture for your own deployment, the runtime itself does not call home and never will. The on-disk and on-wire formats live entirely inside your trust boundary.

Contact

See also EFF DNT-policy compliance. Questions about what we store: open an issue on GitHub. If you find a violation of the above (e.g., a third-party request you didn't expect), it's a bug — email security@asclepius.health.
