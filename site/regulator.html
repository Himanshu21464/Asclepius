What an auditor should look for.

Asclepius is not certified to any single framework — substrates rarely are. But every clause that an auditor cares about maps to a specific, named mechanism in the runtime. This page is that map. If your framework isn't here, send a request — the gap is almost certainly an oversight, not an absence.

§ 01 US · 45 CFR § 164 subparts C+E last reviewed 2026-04-01

HIPAA — Security & Privacy Rules.

HIPAA does not regulate AI directly; it regulates protected health information. When an AI tool sees, derives, or stores PHI, every Security Rule administrative-, physical-, and technical-safeguard clause applies. The substrate addresses the technical safeguards directly and provides the audit substrate for the administrative ones.

requirementAsclepius primitive
§ 164.312(a)(1) · access controlUnique user id, automatic logoff, encryption at rest. Every entry carries an actor id (clin/svc/sys/agent prefix); KeyStore can sit behind an HSM with per-actor delegation. See KeyStore · Deploy.
§ 164.312(b) · audit controlsHardware, software, procedural mechanisms that record and examine activity. The Merkle-chained signed ledger is this control. Every inference, consent change, key event, and bundle export is one signed row. See Ledger · audit-entry schema.
§ 164.312(c)(1) · integrityProtect ePHI from improper alteration or destruction. Append-only chain; prev_hash binds every row to its predecessor. Any retroactive edit fails verification. See Playbook PB-01.
§ 164.312(d) · person/entity authenticationVerify that a person/entity seeking access is the one claimed. Ed25519 sig on every entry binds the action to the keyholder. Two-key co-sign for human-ratified agentic actions. See ADR-001.
§ 164.312(e)(1) · transmission securityGuard ePHI in transit. Substrate is transport-agnostic — operate behind your TLS terminator. Bundles are signed at rest, tamper-evident in transit.
§ 164.524 · access of individualsRight of access to one's PHI. Per-patient bundle export: asclepius bundle export --patient PT-…. The patient (or their delegate) gets a signed, replayable record.
§ 164.508 · authorisations & revocationsTrack consent and its revocation. ConsentRegistry records grant/expire/revoke as signed entries; runtime revalidates scope every inference. See Playbook PB-05.

Substrate posture. Asclepius does not become a covered entity or business associate by virtue of being deployed; the operator does. The substrate is a vendor-neutral library — the BAA is between the operator and the model vendor, not the operator and us.

§ 02 EU · Regulation (EU) 2024/1689 last reviewed 2026-04-01

EU AI Act — high-risk obligations.

Article 6 lists healthcare AI explicitly as high-risk. The Title III obligations — risk management, data governance, technical documentation, record-keeping, transparency, human oversight, robustness, post-market monitoring — are the meat of the audit. The substrate addresses record-keeping and post-market monitoring at the primitive level.

obligationAsclepius primitive
art. 9 · risk managementIterative process; identify, analyse, evaluate, mitigate. Threat model A1–A6 enumerates substrate-level risks; conformance suite L1/L2/L3 demonstrates mitigations. See threat-model · conformance.
art. 10 · data & data governanceTraining/validation/test sets · representativeness · freedom from errors. Out-of-scope at the substrate layer; the operator's MLOps stack governs this. The substrate records the resulting model artefact (weights_sha256) so the data lineage is bound to runtime decisions.
art. 12 · record-keepingLogs that enable traceability of the AI system's functioning over its life. The ledger is this control. Every inference, every drift snapshot, every consent change, every key rotation, is one signed entry. See Ledger.
art. 13 · transparency & provision of information to deployersOperating instructions, performance characteristics, oversight measures. Public spec, threat model, conformance suite, ADR log. See docs · decisions.
art. 14 · human oversightEffective oversight by natural persons; ability to override or stop. ActionFilter blast-radius table forces high-impact actions to require a human co-sign. See agentic use case · agent recipe.
art. 15 · accuracy, robustness, cybersecurityPerformance levels, resilience to unauthorised attempts to alter use. Drift monitor (PSI/KS/EMD), evidence bundles, the threat model's A2–A4 mitigations. See benchmarks · threat model.
art. 17 · post-market monitoringDocument/analyse data on the system in actual use. Drift snapshots and conformance reports are emitted continuously and bound to releases. Bundles can be aggregated into a post-market dossier.

Substrate posture. Asclepius is not itself a high-risk AI system under art. 3 — it is a record-keeping and oversight component. The AI provider remains responsible for arts. 9, 10, 13, 14, 15 conformity; the substrate provides the audit infrastructure that art. 12 and 17 demand.

§ 03 US · 21 CFR § 820 · 510(k) / De Novo last reviewed 2026-04-01

FDA — SaMD pre-market submissions.

Software as a Medical Device with AI/ML components must include a Predetermined Change Control Plan (PCCP), a Good Machine Learning Practice (GMLP) attestation, and post-market real-world performance reporting. The substrate is the mechanism by which an operator demonstrates that the deployed model is the model that was cleared.

obligationAsclepius primitive
21 CFR § 820.30 · design controlsEstablish & maintain procedures. ADR log + spec + threat model + conformance suite are the design history file in plain sight. See decisions · spec.
510(k) · device identityThe cleared device is the device on the market. Every entry binds weights_sha256 of the artefact that produced it. Substrate refuses to commit if the running artefact's hash drifts from the cleared hash. See Runtime.
PCCP · predetermined change controlPermitted changes & their boundary. Policy chain entries are versioned (e.g., phi.v4); a policy or model bump is one signed policy.added or model.updated entry, replayable end to end.
GMLP principles 1–10Total product life-cycle, end-to-end clinical evaluation. Evaluation Harness writes a signed report bundle (batch eval recipe). Drift monitor watches deployed shift.
post-market real-world performanceReportable adverse-event traceability. Encounter-scoped bundle export. The chain is the evidence pack handed to MDR. See Playbook PB-06.

Substrate posture. Asclepius is not a cleared device. It is a record-keeping substrate that helps a cleared device demonstrate continued conformance. The 510(k) holder remains the AI provider — the substrate just makes their post-market evidence trivially defensible.

§ 04 CA · Health & Safety Code § 1339.75 last reviewed 2026-04-01

California AB 3030 — generative AI in clinical comms.

AB 3030 (effective Jan 1, 2025) requires health facilities to disclose when generative AI was used in patient communications and to identify a human contact for clinical-information questions. The substrate provides the verifiable trail that "AI was used here, and here's exactly what it did" — without which the disclosure is just words.

obligationAsclepius primitive
disclosure of GenAI use"AI was used in this communication." Every inference of kind: inference.completed with a generative model is one signed row. The disclosure is mechanically derivable from the chain.
human contactProvide a way to reach a clinician for follow-up. Out-of-substrate; the operator's workflow handles this. The substrate ensures the actor on file is identifiable from the entry.
exemptions for clinician reviewIf a clinician reviewed before patient communication, disclosure may not be required. The chain shows whether a clinician.read entry was bound to the AI's model.suggested output before the message went out. Exemption is provable, not asserted.

Substrate posture. AB 3030 is the cleanest fit on this page — Asclepius makes "did a human review this AI output before it reached the patient?" answerable from the chain alone. Auditable disclosures instead of disclosure-shaped marketing copy.

§ 05 US · The Joint Commission · RC.01.03.01 · IM.02.01.03 last reviewed 2026-04-01

Joint Commission — medical record integrity.

Joint Commission accreditation requires the medical record to be "complete, accurate, and authenticated" with retention policies and a process for late entries / amendments. AI-generated content makes "authenticated" a non-trivial question. The substrate makes it trivial again.

standardAsclepius primitive
RC.01.03.01 EP-2 · authenticationEach entry must be authenticated by the author. Every entry signed by an Ed25519 actor key. Co-signed for agentic actions. See ADR-001.
RC.01.03.01 EP-3 · amendments & correctionsOriginal content, the change, and the reason are preserved. Append-only chain forbids in-place edits. Amendments are new entries with refers_to: seq:N and a stated reason — original survives.
RC.02.01.07 · timelinessEntries reflect the time they were made. ts field is RFC 3339 UTC; ledger refuses out-of-order writes. Late entries carry both their ts and the recorded_at the substrate observed.
IM.02.01.03 · integrity over timeInformation protected against unauthorised access, loss, alteration. Merkle root published nightly to the changelog feed; auditors can reconstruct the historical state from any backup.

What the substrate is not.

Asclepius is not a certification, an attestation, or a regulatory blessing. It is a mechanism — open, vendor-neutral, and reproducible — that makes the obligations enforceable. Auditors should not trust the substrate; they should verify the substrate, with the same tools they use on every other piece of evidence.

If a regulator asks "what does Asclepius do for compliance?" the honest answer is: it shortens the question from "produce a credible audit trail" to "publish the trail you already have."
