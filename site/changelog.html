What ships, and when.

Asclepius follows semver. The runtime, on-disk format, and on-wire schema are stable across the v1.x line; pre-1.0 releases may break compatibility, but every break is annotated below and migrated by a one-shot script.

  • addDriftMonitor::feature_count_observed() — number of registered features with at least one observation in the current window. The "is the pipeline actually feeding the monitor?" probe ahead of a full report(); distinguishes registered-but-silent from registered-and-flowing.
  • addConsentRegistry::find_oldest_token_for_patient(patient) — earliest granted_at across every state for one patient. Per-patient sibling of oldest_active on a different scope: "when did this person first ever appear?".
  • addConsentRegistry::estimated_avg_ttl() — Result<chrono::nanoseconds>; mean lifespan across every token regardless of state. The "how long do consents typically last on this substrate?" cell; not_found on an empty registry.
  • addConsentRegistry::find_longest_lived_for_patient(patient) — widest (expires_at - granted_at) span for one patient across every state. Distinct from longest_lived_active_for_patient (active-only) and longest_active (registry-wide).

  • addInference::ledger_snapshot_seq() — noexcept sugar over seq().value_or(0); returns the committed seq or 0 before commit. The cell a metric label or a trace tag reaches for when "not committed yet" can be encoded as zero.
  • addRuntime::wait_until_chain_grows(min_seq, timeout) — blocking poll. ok on growth past min_seq, timeout when the deadline expires first. Replaces busy-looping has_event_after_seq() on the follower side.
  • addRuntime::record_shutdown(reason) — appends a runtime.shutdown tombstone with reason attached. The signal a forensic operator reads to distinguish an intentional halt from a chain that simply ended.
  • addRuntime::policy_count() — sugar over policies().size(). The cheap cardinality cell a status-bar widget reads without reaching through a sub-component.

  • Notarization adapters (Sigstore, OpenTimestamps)
  • Embedding-drift on top of scalar drift
  • Post-quantum signer (when standardized)
  • Managed evidence service (commercial offering)

§ compatibility guarantees

Patch (0.1.x): bug fixes only. Binary-compatible. No format changes.

Minor (0.x.0): additive changes only. New event types, new fields, new policies. Old verifiers ignore unknown fields.

Major (x.0.0): incompatible changes are allowed. A migration script ships with every major bump (asclepius migrate vN-1_to_vN). Bundles emitted by version N continue to verify with the same major version forever.

v1.0 onward: the on-disk ledger format and on-wire sidecar schema are frozen. Removing or renaming a field is a v2 break and requires a new schema package (asclepius.v2).
