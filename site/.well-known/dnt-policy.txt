Asclepius — Do Not Track policy
================================

This statement complies with the EFF Do Not Track Compliance Policy
(<https://www.eff.org/dnt-policy>) and reflects how asclepius.health treats
visitor activity.

asclepius.health does not track its visitors.

We do not log IP addresses for analytics. We do not set cookies. We do not
embed third-party trackers, A/B testing frameworks, fingerprinting
libraries, or marketing pixels. We do not load any external JavaScript at
runtime. The only third-party request the site issues is to fonts.googleapis.com
to fetch the typefaces it is set in; that request is not subject to our control
but is documented at /cookies.html. If you set the Do Not Track header, the
site behaves identically to the way it behaves when you don't — there is
nothing for us to disable, because there is nothing we collect.

The site does store two preferences in your own browser's localStorage —
your chosen color theme and the in-progress state of the in-browser
playground. Neither is transmitted anywhere. You can clear both at any
time from your browser's site-data panel.

What we keep:
  * standard server access logs as required for security and abuse handling,
    rotated within 14 days, never used for advertising or behavioral
    profiling.
  * exception traces from the build pipeline, never tied to visitor IPs.

What we do not keep:
  * any record of which page a visitor viewed
  * any record of how long a visitor spent on the site
  * any record of which pages link to which other pages they visited
  * any record of a visitor's user-agent for any purpose other than 14-day
    abuse handling

What we will never start doing without a release tag:
  * adding analytics SDKs, marketing pixels, or third-party scripts
  * sharing aggregate or individual data with any third party
  * setting cookies for any purpose other than first-party preference

If a future release ever changes this, the change will appear in the
public changelog (https://asclepius.health/changelog.html) under a
"deprecation of privacy posture" notice with at least 30 days' lead time.
