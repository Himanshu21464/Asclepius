Compare two bundles.

An auditor receives two sequential exports from the same operator. Each one is a JSONL ledger excerpt. Drop them in the panes below and Asclepius diffs by seq and payload_hash — surfacing newly added rows, anything missing, and anything whose body changed since the previous bundle. Retroactive edits show up amber.

L · earlier bundle
R · later bundle
drop two bundles · click diff to compare

What this catches. If the operator silently rewrites seq 442 between two bundles, this page shows that row in amber — same seq, different payload_hash. That's a retroactive edit; if the chain still verifies, the operator either re-signed it or rotated keys, both of which are events you should ask about.

What it does not catch. Anything outside the JSONL. Bundle metadata, signing-key changes, drift snapshots — see verify-bundle for the full ritual.
